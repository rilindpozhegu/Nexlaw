AREAS OF EXPERTISE

NexLaw is a Consultancy engaged in the provision of legal advice and assistance to Shipowners, Charterers, Commodity Traders, Steel Producers, Grain Houses, Underwriters, Shipyards and the Maritime community at large.

NexLaw Areas
of Expertise

Casualty investigation and management

We are specialists in this field having undertaken instructions in excess of 60 ship related casualties since 1997. Please refer to the attached schedule for a list of casualty instructions that we have undertaken in the past.

Piracy in the Gulf of Aden

There are probably only two lawyers globally who have personally engaged and negotiated with the Somali pirates. Ravi is one of them. His experience in personally dealing with the pirates in Somalia’s Puntland region is extensive.

Newbuilding Contracts / Shiprepair Contracts

Once upon a time we were retained by Turkey’s leading Shipbuilder whom we advised and assisted in the sale of no less than 54 newbuilding IMO II & III chemical and product tankers of between 3500 – 17000dwt.

Ship Finance related issues

The fall in asset values has caused much concern not only to shipowners but also many lenders. There are those lenders who claim their borrowers are in breach of their covenant to maintain agreed LTVs.

Commodity Contracts

We are regularly instructed by commodity traders in relation to various disputes arising under their contracts and our speciality areas are scrap metal, steel products, coal and grain products.

Aggregates Trades in Arabian Gulf

The carriage of aggregates (gabbro) and rock from the UAE to Mesaieed, Shuaiba and Basra which is mainly on CQD basis has resulted in a number of disputes in the recent past. We have been instructed in a number of such disputes and have managed to resolve many amicably and swiftly.

Submarine Telecom and Sub-Sea Engineering projects

Cable laying and maintenance projects is one of our speciality areas as is liability disputes relating to damage to sub-sea fibre optic cables. We advise Contractors in relation to the sub-sea engineering contracts and provision of marine related resources. We have advised Clients in relation to the Sakhalin II Project.

Servicing the needs of Shipowners and Ship Operators

We provide shipowners and operators with a professional support structure to not only avoid potential disputes but in the event of a dispute or claim, to secure swift resolution thereof.

Advising Steel Mills

We have been advising a number of steel mills in Turkey since 2001. We are familiar with the entire steel production process and regularly advise and support our Clients in resolving disputes relating to quality/specification, defaults in the supply chain and defaults by buyers.

Litigation

We represent our Clients in LMAA, LCIA and ICC arbitrations. We also instruct Counsel in London to advise and where necessary to draft pleadings.

Mediation

Given Ravi’s involvement in the maritime and commodities trades since 1990, there are many parties who approach him from time to time to act as a mediator. These mediations are undertaken in a very informal manner and the success rate thus far has been 100% (47 mediations to date).
